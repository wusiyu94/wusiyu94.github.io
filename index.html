International Career

Klose is best known for his performances with the Germany national team. He was part of the German team that won the 2014 FIFA World Cup, having previously finished second (2002) and third (2006, 2010) in the competition, and as runner-up in UEFA Euro 2008. He is the top goalscorer in the history of the FIFA World Cup (with 16 goals).

Club Career

At club level, Klose has been a less prolific but usually reliable goalscorer. Starting his career at Homburg, he played in the Kaiserslautern, Bremen and Bayern Munich, and last played at Lazion in Italy. He won two league titles with Bayern, along with cup competitions at Bayern, Werder Bremen and Lazio.

Style of Play

A prolific goalscorer, Klose was a large and powerful striker who was known in particular for his ability in the air as a centre-forward, due to his strength, timing, elevation and heading accuracy, as well as his finishing ability. In addition to his physical attributes, he possessed good technique, and was capable of setting-up goals for teammates in addition to scoring them himself, due to his tactical intelligence and ability to interpret the game. He was also known for his dedication and correct behaviour on the pitch.
